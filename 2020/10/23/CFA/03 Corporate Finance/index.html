1 Brief Introduction

Capital budgeting
The process that companies use for decision making on capital projects-those projects with a life of a year or more（不少于一年的）.

  • Step 1: Generating ideas(2.1)
  • Step 2: Analyzing individual project proposals(策划书).(2.2)
  • Step 3: Planning the capital budget for the firm. (2.4)
  • Step 4: Monitoring decisions and conducting a post-audit（事后审计，复盘）.

2 Capital Budgeting ★

2.1 Types of capital projects

  • Replacement projects
    • projects to maintain the business.
    • projects for cost reduction
  • Expansion projects
  • New products or services
  • Mandatory projects
    • for safety and environment （政府强制）
  • Other projects
    • pet projects（宠物项目：依据与老板的个人兴趣爱好)
    • R&D projects （research &develop）

2.2 Principles of capital budgeting

The after tax incremental cash flows(税后现金流增量) provide a sound basis for capital budgeting. It involves basic principles as follows:（三要两不要，时间更重要）

Inclue

  • Opportunity costs : the foregone return of the resource invests in the next-best project.
  • Externalities(外部因素) :
    • cannibalization (negative) 利益侵蚀(新推出的无糖可乐寝室有糖可乐份额)
    • synergy (positive).协同效应

Exclude

  • Sunk costs – decisions should not be affected by sunk costs.(沉默成本：在项目进行前的咨询，调研，历史采购)
  • Financing costs – they are considered in discount rate (avoiding double counting problem).（融资成本）
  • Timing value of cash flows is crucial.

2.3 Valuation methods for single project

计算，优缺点，NPV vs IRR的矛盾

2.3.1 NPV : Net present value

the sum of the present value of the future after-tax cash flows.

​

计算器联系计算

CPT(计算器的compute按键)

  • If NPV > 0, then accept/invest.
  • If NPV < 0, then reject/not invest.

2.3.2 IRR：Internal rate of return (IRR)(内部收益率)

The discount rate that makes the total present value of all cash flows, the NPV equal to zero. It is the expected returnon the project.

例子：Cost of capital(discount rate) = 10%时

  • If IRR > cost of capital, then accept/invest.
  • If IRR < cost of capital, then reject/not invest.

Multiple and no IRR problem （可能多个解或者无解）

Conventional cash flows: cash flows change signs once.(传统的，只有第一次负数)
Non conventional cash flows: cash flows change signs more
than once.

2.3.3 PI: Profitability index 盈利指数

  • If PI > 1, then accept/invest.
  • If PI < 1, then reject/not invest.

2.3.4 PB: Payback period(投资回收期)

A: the last period with negative cumulative cash flow.
B: the absolute value of cumulative cash flow at the end of the period A.
C: the cash flow during the period after A.

2.3.5 DPB: Discounted payback period

DCF: discount cash flow

DBP>DP

2.3.6 NPV vs IRR vs DP vs DPB vs PI

2.4 Valuation of Interacting Projects

2.4.1 Investment Decision Criteria

Project interaction

  • Independent projects: projects are unrelated to each other.
    NPV method：Accept all projects with NPV>0 (IRR>cost of capital, PI>1).
  • Unlimited funds: the firm can raise the funds it wants for all profitable projects.
  • Mutually exclusive projects(互斥项目): projects compete directly with each other.
  • Capital rationing(资金配额): the firm has a fixed amount of funds to invest.
    NPV method: choose the one with highest NPV.
    IRR method: choose the one with highest IRR.
    PI method: choose the one with highest PI.
  • Project sequencing: projects should be taken in time order.

    2.4.2 Plot

  1. 两个IRR根据项目固定，不会随着discount rate改变
  2. 当二者NPV相等时，cross over rate一定小于最小的
  3. 在crossover 左侧出现矛盾，此时A的NPV更大但IRR固定

2.4.3 NPV and stock price

NPV is a direct measure of the expected change in shareholder wealth from a project. Theoretically:
A project with positive NPV will increase the wealth of shareholder and stock price.
A project with negative NPV will decrease the wealth of shareholder and stock price.
Changes in stock price = NPV/number of shares

3 Cost of Capital

3.1 Weighted Average Cost of Capital

★加权平均资金成本

Cost of capital is The rate of return that the suppliers of capital require as compensation for their contribution of capital, or the opportunity cost of funds for the suppliers of capital.

A company can raise capital from:

  • Debt
  • Common equity
  • Preferred stock

to calculate the margin costs of each sources of capital and then calculate a weighted average of these costs

w = the proportion of each resource for the company to raises
new funds, and should be base on the market value（而非book value）.
kd= the before-tax marginal cost of debt.
kps= the marginal cost of preferred stock.
kce = the marginal cost of common equity.
t = the company’s marginal tax rate.

(税盾) :the higher the tax rate, the lower the debt cost , the lower the WACC.

If target capital structure is not available, estimate weights using one of the several approaches:

  • The company’s current capital structure.
  • Infer the target capital structure by trends in the company’s capital structure or statements by management.
  • Use averages of comparable companies’ capital structures.

3.2 Application of WACC

3.2.1 Capital-budgeting decision making

  1. Decide the optimal capital budget.
    the intersection of MCC curve and IOS curve

Marginal cost of capital (MCC=WACC): the cost of an additional dollar of capital. MCC generally increases as the firm increases the capital raised.
Investment opportunity schedule (IOS): returns to a company’s investment opportunities generally decrease as the company make additional investments.

一定要在左侧

WACC(MCC) adjusted appropriately for the risk of a given project

When using the company’s WACC as discount rate in the calculation of the NPV of a project, there are two implied assumptions:

  1. The project has the average-risk of the company.
  • If project’s risk > average-risk: discount rate will be underestimated, and NPV will be overestimated.
  • If project’s risk < average-risk: discount rate will be overestimated, and NPV will be underestimated.
  1. The company will have a constant target capital structure throughout its useful life.

3.4 Issues in Cost of Capital

3.4.1 beta

Factors affecting the beta (βEquity) of a company’s stock:

  • Systematic components of business risk.
    • Sales risk: risk related to the uncertainty of revenues
    • Operating risk: risk attributed to fixed cost of operation.
  • Financial risk: risk attributed to fixed cost of financing (e.g. interest of debt and lease).

pure-play methodusing a publicly traded comparable company’s stock beta

  1. Step 1: select the comparable company (publicly traded, similar business risk).
  2. Step 2: estimate comparable’s stock beta (βEquity, Comp.).
  3. Step 3: Un-leverthe comparable’s stock beta, remove the effects of financial leverage, then have the comparable’s asset beta (βAsset, Comp.).
  4. Step 4: Re-lever the asset beta, then we have the project’s stock beta (βEquity, Proj.).

Un-lever

D/E: debt-to-equity ratio of comparable(market average)

Re-lever

D’/E’: debt-to-equity ratio of project(expected ratio)

3.4.2 Marginal cost of capital schedule

When calculate CAPM of a developing country , CPR(country risk premium国家风险溢价) should be added due to beta does not adequately capture country risk for companies in developing countries

Sovereign yield spread: difference between the yield of government bonds in the developing country( denominated in the currency of a developed country) and Treasury bonds yield with similar maturity in the developed country.

Treasury bonds:也是国债，但T-bond一般都是美国债。

3.4.3 break-points

Break point: the amount of capital at which the WACC changes, which means that the cost of one of the sources of capital change

3.4.4 flotation costs

Flotation cost : the fee charged by investment bankers when a company raises additional capital.

  • For debt and preferred stock, flotation cost is quite small (<1%), and usually do not incorporate in the estimated cost of capital.

  • For equity, the flotation cost may be substantial, and should be considered when estimating the cost of external equity capital. Deduct the flotation cost from the initial cash flow of projects

04 Measures of Leverage

explain leverage

classify business risk, sales risk, operating risk, and financial risk.

4.1 definition

A given change in one variable leads to a greater change in other variable because of fixed costLeverage increases the volatility of a company’s earnings and cash flows (both up and down), and increases the risk of lending to (creditors) or owning a company (shareholders)

Operating leveragecreated by fixed operating cost (e.g. depreciation and rent)

Financial leveragecreated by fixed financial cost (e.g. interest)

4.2 Risk

  • Business risk
    • Sales risk :uncertainty with respect to the price and quantity of goods and services(行业不受自己控制)
    • Operating risk:risk attributed to the use of fixed cost in operation (e.g., rent, depreciation)
      The greater the fixed operating costs relative to variable operating costs, the greater the operating risk(固定成本占比越高，经营分线越大)
  • Financial risk：the risk associated with how a company finances its operations
    The more fixed-cost financial obligations (e.g. debt, long-term leases), the greater the financial risk

5 Working Capital Management

5.1 Sources of liquidity

Primary

  • Ready cash balances.
  • Short-term funds: trade credit, bank lines of credit, short-term investment portfolio.
  • Cash flow management
    Secondary
  • Negotiating debt contract.
  • Liquidating assets.
  • Filing for bankruptcy protection and reorganization

5.2 Drags and pulls on liquidity

Drags on liquidity: delay or reduce cashinflows, or increase borrowing cost.
Uncollected receivables; obsolete inventory; tight credit.

Pulls on Liquidity: accelerate cash outflows.
Making payment early; reduced credit limits; limits on short- term lines of credit.

Both drags and pulls reduce the net cash position of a firm.

6.1 Stakeholder

Shareholder theory: the most important responsibility of a company’s managers to maximize shareholder returns.

Stakeholder theory focuses on the interests of not only its shareholders, but also its customers, suppliers, employees, and others who have an interest in the company

  • Shareholders
  • Board of directors
  • Managers and employees
  • Creditors
  • Customers
  • Governments/regulators
